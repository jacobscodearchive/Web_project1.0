HOW BAD IS IT?

It all started in 1907,the first synthetic plastic produces for commercial use. Since then plastics have been more or less part of our daily life. Today the world produces more than 300 Million metric tone of plastic each year. Perhaps in 2020 alone 367 million tons of plastic has been produced which is expected to rise four times more in the coming years. The most concerning thing about this is that only fraction of this plastic ends up in recycling plants the rest goes to land fills, urban living enviroments and our precious rivers and oceans daring the existance of hundreds of species in the marine ecosystem. In addition to the pollution in the marine ecosystem plastics also pose a risk to the human health. we are now seeing plastic in our , our food, soil and air. Plastic particles have been found in every thing from honey, beer, to salt and tea.

WHAT CAN WE DO?

With all the destruction platics are causing to the world you might say why not just Ban plastic production at all. Studies after studies has shown that banning the production of plastic will drive consumers to other less sustainable materials and so has little to no impact. Instead cutting on the scale of the plastic being produced each year can be considered as more realistic approach and Of course it is. Many european countries are implementing this technique. But it seems that the most sustainable and long term solution is recycling but as I mentioned above only 9% of the plastic produced since 1950 have been recycled. By now you might even be thinking that this is just a DEAD END. But wait because we have a better solution.

Welcome to Green House
We promise to take every day use and throw plastic and convert it in to cheap, modern and recyclable houses. This concept was first developed by a brilliant entrepreneure and architect called Oscar Anders Mendez the co-founder of conceptos plasticos. Oscar Mendez came up with a brilliant idea of recycling use and throw pastics like water bottle and many other types of plastic and convert them into strong and resilient structures that can be then used to build houses that now millions of people are living in. Countries like Colombia, Kenya, Nigeria and many more have been using this method to provide houses that can and are:

  • Extremenly cost effective.
  • Withstand strong winds and earth quicks.
  • Long lasting.
  • Recyclable
  • And can also be disassembled and transported to different location.

This kinds of houses can be built in a matter of weeks Perhaps days. We can use them to provide houses for millions of people living on the street, build hundreds of schools in rural areas with cheap labour and material cost. Reconstruct damaged infrastructures and more. We can do a lot with this technology. Generally we can make housing much more affordable for millions.
